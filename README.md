# Systematic Review Matrix
PRISMA Review - "Consumer Adoption of AI Assistants: Factors Influencing Trust, Use, and Perceived Value" Master Thesis

|Author, Year|Title|Brief Summary|
|-----------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **[Acosta-Enriquez et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.heliyon.2024.e38315)** | Acceptance of artificial intelligence in university contexts: A conceptual analysis based on UTAUT2 theory | Examines AI acceptance in academia using UTAUT2, emphasizing how educational environments shape adoption drivers. |
| **[Affandi et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jretconser.2024.104087)** | AI assistant is my new best friend! Role of emotional disclosure, performance expectations and intention to reuse | Analyzes the role of emotional connection and performance expectations in reuse intention. |
| **[Afroogh et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1057/s41599-024-04044-8)** | Trust in AI: Progress, challenges, and future directions | Provides a comprehensive review of the evolution of trust in AI, identifying current barriers and multi-dimensional trust frameworks. |
| **[Al-Abdullatif & Alsubaie, 2024](https://doi.org/10.3390/bs14090845)** | ChatGPT in Learning: Assessing Students’ Use Intentions through the Lens of Perceived Value and the Influence of AI Literacy | Links AI literacy to perceived value, showing that a user’s knowledge of AI significantly impacts their assessment of its utility. |
| **[Al Mamun et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.17705/1thci.00216)** | What Drives User Intention to Continue Using Conversational AI? | Analyzes how functional and emotional values contribute to the long-term continuance intention of conversational AI users. |
| **[Ashrafi & Easmin, 2023](https://doi.org/10.1142/S0219877023500049)** | Okay Google, good to talk to you… Examining the determinants affecting users’ behavioral intention for adopting voice assistants | Investigates the role of self-efficacy and other determinants in adopting voice technology. |
| **[Chen et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1145/3622782)** | User experience of digital voice assistant: Conceptualization and measurement | Develops and validates a framework for measuring how users experience digital voice assistants. |
| **[S. Chen et al., 2026](https://doi.org/10.1108/APJML-06-2024-0778)** | How does AI technological uncertainty affect continuance usage intention of Voice AI Assistants? A valance-based framework | Evaluates the impact of technical uncertainty on long-term user retention for voice assistants. |
| **[Choi & Drumwright, 2021](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tele.2021.101628)** | “OK, Google, why do I use you?” Motivations, post‑consumption evaluations, and perceptions of voice AI assistants | Explores motivations and evaluations following the use of voice assistants like Google Assistant. |
| **[Choudhary et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1080/08874417.2024.2312858)** | Assessing Factors Influencing Customers’ Adoption of AI-Based Voice Assistants | Evaluates the key determinants influencing consumers to adopt AI-powered voice assistants in their daily lives. |
| **[De Matos et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1108/IJBM-05-2025-0403)** | AI-induced privacy concerns: examining the moderating effect of AI fear on customer data sharing | Explores how “AI fear” intensifies privacy concerns, acting as a moderator that hinders data sharing and adoption. |
| **[García De Blanes Sebastián et al., 2022](https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2022.993935)** | Application and extension of the UTAUT2 model for determining behavioral intention factors in use of the artificial intelligence virtual assistants | Validates UTAUT2 in the specific context of virtual assistants (e.g., Alexa), confirming ease of use and social influence as key drivers. |
| **[Ghazi et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1177/14673584231184161)** | Benefit-triggered or trust-guided? Investigation of customers’ perceptions towards AI-adopting hotels amid and post COVID-19 pandemic | Investigates the tension between functional benefits and trust-based guidance in the service industry during periods of crisis. |
| **[Guzman & Lewis, 2020](https://doi.org/10.1177/1461444819858691)** | Artificial intelligence and communication: A Human–Machine Communication research agenda | Frames AI as social communicators instead of tools, theorizing how relational exchange and trust drive technology adoption. |
| **[Hanshaw & Sullivan, 2025](https://doi.org/10.1007/s44163-025-00312-x)** | Exploring barriers to AI course assistant adoption: A mixed-methods study on student non-utilization | Identifies why students choose not to use AI assistants in an educational setting. |
| **[Huy et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.17705/1thci.00198)** | Generative AI, Why, How, and Outcomes: A User Adoption Study | Examines the motivations, methods of use, and resulting outcomes of generative AI adoption among users. |
| **[Jain et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1002/cb.2233)** | Artificial intelligence consumer behavior: A hybrid review and research agenda | Offers a state-of-the-art review of AI consumer behavior, proposing a research agenda focused on the psychological impact of AI. |
| **[Johnson & Reimer, 2023](https://doi.org/10.3390/su15129224)** | The Adoption and Use of Smart Assistants in Residential Homes: The Matching Hypothesis | Proposes the “Matching Hypothesis”, where adoption depends on the fit between assistant capabilities and the user’s specific home needs. |
| **[Kaur et al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.1145/3491209)** | Trustworthy Artificial Intelligence: A Review | Provides a comprehensive review of the frameworks and requirements necessary to build and maintain trust in AI systems. |
| **[Khanfar et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1108/MD-05-2023-0838)** | Factors influencing the adoption of artificial intelligence systems: A systematic literature review | A high-level meta-analysis identifying cross-industry factors that consistently drive or inhibit AI system adoption. |
| **[Li & Wei, 2025](https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1618689)** | Factors influencing older adults’ adoption of AI voice assistants: extending the UTAUT model | Examines age as a critical demographic factor, showing how simplicity and value perception vary for older cohorts. |
| **[X. Li et al., 2026](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.teler.2026.100293)** | Influencing factors of user acceptance and adoption of e-government AI assistants | Analyzes adoption drivers of AI assistants within the public sector context using an extended UTAUT2 model. |
| **[Mirbabaie et al., 2022](https://doi.org/10.17705/1CAIS.05034)** | Ethics and AI in Information Systems Research | Explores the ethical implications and research directions for AI within the Information Systems discipline. |
| **[Mishra et al., 2022](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijinfomgt.2021.102413)** | Psychological determinants of users’ adoption and word-of-mouth recommendations of smart voice assistants | Examines psychological drivers of adoption and subsequent peer recommendations. |
| **[Pal et al., 2021](https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2021.3132399)** | Exploring the determinants of users’ continuance usage intention of smart voice assistants | Identifies the factors that determine whether a user will continue using a smart voice assistant. |
| **[Pandey & Rai, 2023](https://doi.org/10.1177/23197145231196066)** | Consumer Adoption of AI-powered Virtual Assistants (AIVA): An Integrated Model Based on the SEM–ANN Approach | Uses a hybrid SEM-ANN approach to model consumer adoption of virtual assistants. |
| **[Rana et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.heliyon.2024.e37569)** | Assessing AI adoption in developing country academia: A trust and privacy-augmented UTAUT framework | Tailors the UTAUT framework to include trust and privacy as primary pillars for technology adoption in developing regions. |
| **[Salih et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1080/08874417.2025.2452544)** | AI-Enabled Service Continuance: Roles of Trust and Privacy Risk | Focuses on “post-adoption” behavior, arguing that trust and privacy risk are the main factors in whether a user continues using AI. |
| **[Shao et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1080/0960085X.2024.2363322)** | Uncovering post-adoption usage of AI-based voice assistants: a technology affordance lens using a mixed-methods approach | Examines how technology affordances shape user behavior after the initial adoption of voice assistants. |
| **[Sun, 2021](https://doi.org/10.3390/app112210618)** | Analyzing determinants for adoption of intelligent personal assistant: An empirical study | Conducts an empirical study on the primary determinants that drive the adoption of intelligent personal assistants. |
| **[Tam et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.17645/mac.9534)** | Support for Businesses’ Use of Artificial Intelligence: Dynamics of Trust, Distrust, and Perceived Benefits | Analyzes the interplay between benefits and the “dual-factor” of trust and distrust in professional and business contexts. |
| **[Usiña-Báscones et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2025.3598009)** | Exploring the Moderating Effect of Price on the Relationship Between Intention and Use of Voice Assistants | Examines how price sensitivity influences the transition from intention to actual usage behavior in voice assistant technology. |
| **[Vimalkumar et al., 2021](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chb.2021.106763)** | ‘Okay google, what about my privacy?’: User’s privacy perceptions and acceptance of voice based digital assistants | One of the first major studies to link specific privacy perceptions to the rejection or acceptance of voice-activated AI. |
| **[Wu & Dong, 2025](https://doi.org/10.1109/ACCESS.2025.3544489)** | What Motivates Second Language Majors to Use Generative AI for Informal Learning? Insights From the Theory of Planned Behavior | Applies TPB to understand the motivational drivers behind language students adopting Generative AI for self-directed study. |
| **[Xie et al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.1080/08874417.2023.2167134)** | Understanding continued use intention of AI assistants | Identifies the factors that lead users to maintain long-term usage of AI assistants rather than abandoning them. |
| **[Xu et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.48550/ARXIV.2509.08554)** | Acceptability of AI Assistants for Privacy: Perceptions of Experts and Users on Personalized Privacy Assistants | Compares expert and user perspectives on using AI to manage and protect digital privacy. |
| **[Zhan et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1145/3637339)** | Healthcare Voice AI Assistants: Factors Influencing Trust and Intention to Use | Focuses on the specific trust dynamics and usage intentions regarding AI assistants in a healthcare context. |
